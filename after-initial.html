Following your initial meeting with the student, take a new minutes to finish your Point of Contact (POC) in Ventus:

  • Briefly describe the point of discussion as well as the outcome of your meeeting. Which accommodations did you establish and why? What is the duration of the accommodation plan? Did you provide the student with any letters of support?
  • Did you discuss a learning strategy plan? Referrals to campus resources?

Once complete, don't forget to lock your POC! Remember, once you lock your POC you cannot modify its content. However, you could always append the interaction.

A few more things to consider...

Complete any follow-up actions as agreed or needed. Do you need to consult with the exams team? Do you need to consult with the Coordinator or Manager? Do you have to submit a referral to the Adapted Technology Centre? Begin gathering information for a bursary application?

Lastly, change the event status of the student's appointment to Completed on your Ventus calendar. To do so, click on the student's appointment, event status and select follow up complete.

Follow-up meetings

You did it! You've completed the initial meeting and changed its event status to follow up complete, now what? Do you ever meet with the student again? As part of the accommodation plan, regular check-ins, also known as follow-ups, are recommended to ensure that your student is supported throughout the first few months or their academic accommodation journey. You may schedule follow-up appointments for the following:

  • Modifying or renewing academic accommodation plans: Whether the student has updated documentation, no longer requires a support, or needs to make changes to current supports.
  • Learning Strategies: Perhaps the student wishes to come up with a plan to organize their materials, course notes, or work on exam study strategies.
  • Bursaries: The need to discuss bursary supports for disability-related assistive technology or services such as note takers, tutors, educational assistants.

Follow-up meetings can be scheduled weekly, bi-weekly, monthly, or on an as-need basis. However, it is important that you and your students think proactively instead of reactively, as this will help you both in the long run should any situation arise (ex: students not confirming exams on time).
