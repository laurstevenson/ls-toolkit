Following your initial meeting with the student, take a new minutes to finish yourPoint of Contact (POC) in Ventus:

  • Briefly describe the point of discussion as well as the outcome of your meeeting. Which accommodations did you establish and why? What is the duration of the accommodation plan? Did you provide the student with any letters of support?
  • Did you discuss a learning strategy plan? Referrals to campus resources?

Once complete, don't forget to lock your POC! Remember, once you lock your POC you cannot modify its content. However, you could always append the interaction.

A few more things to consider...

Complete any follow-up actions as agreed or needed. Do you need to consult with the exams team? Do you need to consult with the Coordinator or Manager? Do you have to submit a referral to the Adapted Technology Centre? Begin gathering information for a bursary application?

Lastly, change the event status of the student's appointment to Completed on your Ventus calendar. To do so, click on the student's appointment, event status and select follow up complete.

Follow-up meetings

After reviewing the disability documentation in the Disabilities Tab on Ventus, determine if the disability is primary or secondary. If you're unsure, don't hesitate to ask the student to confirm. Then, you'll need to determine if the disability/condition is either permanent or temporary - Why is this important?

  • If the disability/condition is permanent, and the documentation is up to date, you can consider establishing the academic accommodation plan for the duration of their studies at UO (4-5 years)
  • If the documentation is up to date, but the medical specialist checked off "temporary/duration unknown", you will establish accommodations for one semester (unless there is a specific date written). The student will need to provide you with updated documentation for future terms.
  • If the medical documentation is outdated, interim acccommodations will be in place for one semester while the student seeks new documentation.

Mark the disability category (or categories) and supporting medical documentation as verified. Include an expiry date, if applicable.
