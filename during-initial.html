Engage in a collaborative discussion with the student

Here, you'll discuss disability-related needs in a university context. It is also important to set expectations and discuss differences between secondary and post-secondary studies, as accommodation plans could vary.

To help you in determining an appropriate accommodation plan, it is important to ask a lot of questions. Don't be shy! This is by no means an exhaustive list of questions you could ask. The possibilities are endless! As a starting point, begin with...

  • Have you received accommodations in the past? If yes, which ones? Which were the most helpful and why?
  • What (disability-related) challenges are you facing?
  • What has your experience been like in the classroom environment? What strategies do you use to stay organized, manage your time, study, take notes?
  • What has your experience been like in the exam environment? What strategies do you use to manage stress levels, keep track of time, remain focused?
  • Side effects from medications? Sleep schedule/Daily routine

Make sure that you write down important information that will help you in creating the accommodation plan and to justify why these accommodations were chosen.

Establishing Academic Accommodations

Remember, accommodations approved only for students with disabilities on an individualized basis. Academic accommodations are specific to three factors:

  • a student's functional limitations stemming from their disability as verified by disability documentation
  • the student's own experience of their disability at university, and,
  • the academic requirements of their course or program.

Establish Exam Accommodations

After you have agreed on the exam accommodation plan, you'll need to activate these supports via the 'Exam Accommodations' tab on Ventus. Select on the exam accommodations and enter an expiry date. Make sure that the Exam Accommodations are saved and active (should appear as green)

As a reminder, temporary accommodations are typically established for one semester. Permanent supports are typically established for the expected duration of their program. However, you may choose to establish and revisit accommodation supports each year, if that's the case enter an expiry date after one full academic year and explain to the student the justification.

Establish Teaching Accommodations

These include, but are not limited to, Transcription, Library, Adapted Furniture and Other accommodations.

  • Go to the Template Teaching Accommodations page on Ventus
  • Uncheck Hide disabled accommodations, this will allow you to view all teaching accommodations while you familiarize yourself with the specific names of supports
  • In the search bar, type in the teaching accommodations you wish to establish.
  • Enable the the chosen accommodation
  • Select whether or not the support will be Self-Serve or not. If the accommodation is self-servem the student does not need to meet with you to review accommoation procedures prior to approval. If not self-serve, the student will need to contact their LS to review accommodation procedures prior to approval
  • Set an expiry date and Save

Review exam procedures

In short, students have the option to write with or without established exam accommodations. The procedure, outlined in greater detail on our website, is as follows:

  • At least 10 calendar days prior to a test, professors/faculties submit a Notice of Examination in their Ventus portal
  • Students receive an email from Ventus asking them to confirm their participation and accommodations
  • Students have to confirm each exam by the following deadlines: seven days prior to each midterm/quiz/deferred exam, November 15th for Fall term final exams, March 15th for Winter term final exams

If a student forgets to confirm an exam, there is no guarantee that an exception could be made. Encourage your students to frequently check their emails and booksmart Ventus on their browser.
